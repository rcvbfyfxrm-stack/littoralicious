Provisioning Miami & Fort Lauderdale: The Complete Playbook

The best provisioning city in the western hemisphere. Zero import tax, massive wholesale infrastructure, every cuisine represented. This is the complete supplier database and money-saving playbook — built from working yacht chef intel.

The Brief

Port type: Major hub. Agent markup in this port runs 15–18%. On a USD 15,000 provision, self-sourcing saves USD 2,200–2,700. The wholesale infrastructure is so good here that using an agent is paying someone to do what you can do better yourself — unless you genuinely have zero time.

Costco Business Center is not regular Costco. The Business Center in Doral (also Pompano Beach) carries restaurant-format packaging — 5 kg flour bags, case lots, portion-controlled proteins. Make the extra 20-minute drive. This distinction alone changes your entire provision. Regular Costco sells consumer packs. The Business Center sells professional packs. The difference in value and format is significant.

Miami has zero import tax, multiple wholesale clubs that grant free membership with boat registration, and suppliers covering every quality tier from crew-grade to A5 Wagyu. If your season starts here or you transit through, load everything you can carry. Prices only go up from here.

Restaurant Depot membership is free. Bring boat registration, a business card, and the captain’s ID. One card covers every Restaurant Depot in the US. Between Costco Business Center and Restaurant Depot, you cover 60–70% of any provision at wholesale prices.

How to Provision

Plan ahead. The day before a trip is not for shopping — it is for finalising preparations and cooking ahead. If you are running around shops the day before guests arrive, you have already lost.

If you are sole chef, separate your orders by category across different days. One day for proteins — order from your butcher and fishmonger separately, let them prepare your cuts and portions. Another day for dry store — the wholesale run where you load flour, oils, pasta, canned goods, supplies. Another day for fresh produce and specialty items. Each run has a different rhythm and different suppliers. Trying to do everything in one day means you rush the decisions that matter most.

If you have a team, split the runs. One person on proteins, one on dry store and produce. Same principle: separate by category, not by urgency.

The rule: Your last day before departure is for prep and cook-ahead only. Stocks simmering, sauces reducing, bread proofing, mise en place locked in. Not for standing in a queue at Restaurant Depot. Plan your shopping so you never have to choose between sourcing and cooking on the same day.

Freezer-Ready Intel

What to ask every protein supplier: (1) Can you vac-pack? (2) Can you portion — whole fillet AND individual cuts? (3) What lead time for packed orders? (4) Blast chiller available? A supplier who vac-packs and blast-chills is worth a 10% premium.

Buy here, freeze for later: Wild Fork and Snake River Farms arrive already frozen and vac-packed — best quality for freezer loading. For proteins heading to the Caribbean, these two are your cleanest freezer-ready option.

Do not freeze from here: Fish. Buy fish fresh at the next port. Frozen fish from Miami competes poorly with fresh local catch in the Caribbean.

Logistics and delivery: Uber XL (USD 20–40 per trip to marina, phone ahead, have orders bagged and ready). Uber Direct (express courier, 30 min–2 hours in FTL area). Reliable Couriers knows every marina slip in Lauderdale — Pier 66, Bahia Mar, 17th Street, Las Olas.

Use the agent only when your charter starts in 24 hours and you have not left the boat. Miami’s wholesale infrastructure is so accessible that the agent markup is harder to justify here than anywhere else.

Red flags: Any agent who cannot tell you exactly where they source proteins. If they are vague, they are marking up Restaurant Depot.

The Bottom Line

Miami is the provisioning capital of the Americas. Zero import tax, wholesale infrastructure everywhere, every cuisine and quality tier covered from crew-grade to A5 Wagyu. The single best move is getting the Costco Business Center and Restaurant Depot memberships on day one — between them, they cover 60–70% of any provision at wholesale prices. Before you leave, load flour, Asian pantry staples, and frozen vac-packed proteins for the next three ports. Prices and suppliers shift season to season — verify before committing.
